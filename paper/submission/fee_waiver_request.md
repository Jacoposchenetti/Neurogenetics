# Article processing charge — waiver request

*(Template. Most open-access publishers have a formal waiver or fee-assistance route;
the request is normally made **at submission**, not after acceptance. Check the
current policy and exact wording required by the venue before sending — some use a
form field rather than a letter.)*

---

[Date]

Dear Editors / Publications Office,

I am requesting a full waiver of the article processing charge for my submission,
**"A significant enrichment that is not: spatial nulls, co-expression, and the
imaging transcriptomics of EEG oscillatory-power genetics."**

I am an **independent researcher with no institutional affiliation and no research
funding**. This study received no external, institutional or departmental support of
any kind, and there is no grant, library agreement or institutional membership
against which a publication charge could be billed. I have no means of paying an
article processing charge.

The work was carried out entirely with publicly available secondary data — ENIGMA-EEG
GWAS summary statistics, the Allen Human Brain Atlas, and open reference panels and
parcellations — precisely because this route requires no funding for data collection.
The analysis code, derived results and manuscript source are openly released under an
MIT licence at https://github.com/Jacoposchenetti/Neurogenetics, and the study is
fully reproducible from that repository.

I would add that the manuscript reports a **null result together with a methodological
caution**. Findings of this kind are systematically under-published relative to
positive results, and are disproportionately unlikely to be produced by funded
programmes with positive-outcome incentives. A fee barrier at this point would tend to
select against exactly the category of work that your journal's policy on negative and
methodological results exists to support.

I would be grateful for your consideration, and I am happy to provide any further
information you require.

## Practical notes

- **Ask at submission.** Waivers are normally decided at or before editorial
  assessment; requesting after acceptance is harder and at some venues not possible.
- **Editorial independence.** Reputable publishers keep waiver decisions separate
  from peer review — requesting one does not affect how the paper is assessed, and
  editors do not see it during review.
- **Venue-specific routes.**
  - *PLOS* — Publication Fee Assistance is a formal application; apply during
    submission.
  - *Imaging Neuroscience (MIT Press)* — has a waiver route for authors unable to
    pay; state the lack of affiliation and funding explicitly.
  - *Aperture Neuro (OHBM)* — low charge, with reductions; contact the editorial
    office directly.
  - *Peer Community Journal* — **no charge at all** (diamond open access); no waiver
    needed. Worth keeping as the fallback that removes the problem entirely.
- **Verify current fees** before choosing a venue; they change, and published figures
  go out of date quickly.
