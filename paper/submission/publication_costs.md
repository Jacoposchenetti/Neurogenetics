# Covering the article processing charge

The author holds a collaboration contract (CoCoCo) with CIMeC, University of Trento,
and lists that affiliation on the manuscript. The research itself received no
external or project funding. That combination means the routes below should be tried
**in order** — the first one costs nothing and requires no special pleading.

---

## Route 1 (try first) — institutional open-access agreement

Italian universities participate in **CRUI-CARE** transformative agreements, which
cover or discount article processing charges with many publishers for corresponding
authors affiliated with the institution. University of Trento has an open-access
service that administers this.

**What to do:** before choosing a venue, contact the University of Trento library /
open-access office and ask:

1. Is [journal name] covered by a CRUI-CARE or other institutional agreement?
2. Does eligibility require the corresponding author to be affiliated — and does a
   CoCoCo contract satisfy that requirement?
3. Is prior authorisation needed, and if so what is the procedure and lead time?

Question 2 matters: eligibility is usually defined as "corresponding author
affiliated with the institution", but whether a collaboration contract counts is a
local administrative decision. Ask explicitly rather than assuming — and ask *before*
submitting, since several agreements require the article to be flagged at acceptance.

This is not a favour to request. It is a service the institution has already paid
for.

---

## Route 2 — venues with no charge at all

If no agreement covers the target journal, the problem disappears entirely at a
diamond open-access venue, where publishing and reading are both free:

- **Peer Community Journal** (via Peer Community In) — no charge, rigorous peer
  review, well suited to a study whose strength is openness and reproducibility.

This is a genuine option, not a consolation prize, and worth considering on its
merits rather than only as a fallback.

---

## Route 3 — waiver request

Only if Routes 1 and 2 are unsuitable. Note that the waiver argument must be stated
**accurately**: the author *is* institutionally affiliated. The honest case is not
"no affiliation" but "no research funding against which a charge can be billed".

### Draft

[Date]

Dear Editors / Publications Office,

I am requesting a waiver of the article processing charge for my submission,
**"A significant enrichment that is not: spatial nulls, co-expression, and the
imaging transcriptomics of EEG alpha-power genetics."**

I hold a collaboration contract with the Center for Mind/Brain Sciences, University
of Trento, and list that affiliation on the manuscript. However, this study was
carried out independently of that contract and **received no external, project or
institutional research funding**. There is no grant, project budget or departmental
allocation against which a publication charge can be billed, and I have confirmed
with my institution that [no agreement covers this journal / a CoCoCo contract does
not confer eligibility under the applicable agreement]. I have no personal means of
paying an article processing charge.

The work uses only publicly available secondary data — ENIGMA-EEG GWAS summary
statistics, the Allen Human Brain Atlas, and open reference panels and parcellations
— and the full analysis code and derived results are openly released under an MIT
licence at https://github.com/Jacoposchenetti/Neurogenetics, from which every figure
and reported value can be regenerated.

I would add that the manuscript reports a null result together with a methodological
caution. Work of this kind is systematically under-published relative to positive
findings, and is disproportionately unlikely to originate from funded programmes. I
would be grateful for your consideration.

Yours sincerely,

Jacopo Schenetti
Center for Mind/Brain Sciences (CIMeC), University of Trento, Rovereto, Italy
jacopo.schenetti@unitn.it · ORCID 0009-0000-9108-3806

---

## Practical notes

- **Complete the bracketed clause honestly** — send the waiver request only after
  actually checking with the library, and state what you were told.
- **Ask at submission.** Waivers are normally decided at or before editorial
  assessment; requesting after acceptance is harder or impossible.
- **Editorial independence.** Reputable publishers keep waiver decisions separate
  from peer review; editors do not see the request during assessment.
- **Verify current fees and agreements** before committing to a venue — both change.
